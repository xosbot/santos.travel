## Executive Summary

India's inbound tourism market is in a strong post-pandemic recovery, with **9.95 million foreign tourist arrivals (FTAs) in 2024** and international visitor spending reaching **US$36.8 billion**. The Government of India's Vision@2047 target is **100 million inbound tourists by 2047**, while industry forecasts see FTAs hitting **30.5 million by 2028**.

South India — comprising Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana — is a disproportionately large domestic tourism bloc and an emerging inbound leisure corridor. Kerala alone accounted for **0.73 million FTAs (3.5% of India)** in 2024, while Tamil Nadu captured **1.16 million FTAs (5.5%)**.

For a boutique, premium FIT operator such as **santos.travel**, the addressable opportunity is not the entire India inbound market, but the high-yield slice of independent travelers seeking experiential, heritage, wellness and backwater itineraries in Kerala and South India.

| Market Layer | Definition | Estimated Size (2024/25) |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM** | Total international visitor spending in India | **US$36.8–37.5 bn** |
| **SAM** | Premium/FIT leisure spending in South India (inbound) | **US$8–12 bn** |
| **SOM** | Realistic share capturable by santos.travel (near-term) | **US$5–15 mn** |

## 4. Premium / FIT Segment Sizing

### 4.1 Defining the Premium FIT Segment

For santos.travel, the target customer is:
- **Free Independent Traveler (FIT)** or small private group (2–8 pax).
- High-net-worth individuals, couples, families or small affinity groups.
- Seeking curated experiential itineraries: backwaters, ayurveda/wellness, heritage, wildlife, cuisine, art.
- Long-haul source markets: US, UK, EU, Australia, Canada.
- Average trip length: **10–14 nights**.
- Average spend per person per day: **US$300–600** for premium/luxury.
- Estimated per-trip spend: **US$3,000–8,400 per person**.

### 4.3 Estimated Premium Inbound Spend in South India

**Approach 1: Share-of-spend method**

- Total India international visitor spend 2024: **US$36.8 bn**.
- Estimated share accruing to South India: **~25–30%** based on FTA + domestic mix.
  - Implied South India inbound spend: **US$9.2–11.0 bn**.
- Premium/FIT segment (top 15–20% by spend): **US$1.4–2.2 bn**.

**Approach 2: Visitor-volume × spend method**

- Estimated South India FTAs: **~2.89 million**.
- Average inbound spend per FTA in India: **US$3,700**.
- South India total inbound spend: **~US$10.7 bn**.
- Premium travelers (top 20% by spend, assumed to spend 2.5× average): **~0.58 million visitors** generating **~US$5.4 bn**.

**Conservative premium/FIT market estimate for South India:** **US$4–7 bn**.

Including domestic premium travelers (high-net-worth Indians traveling within South India) and wellness/medical-value travelers, the addressable premium travel market in South India expands to roughly **US$8–12 billion**.

## 5. TAM / SAM / SOM Calculations

### 5.1 Methodology & Assumptions

| Variable | Assumption | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| TAM base | US$36.8 bn | Total international visitor spending in India (2024) |
| South India FTA share | ~29% | TN + Kerala + estimated KA/AP/TS |
| South India inbound spend | ~US$10.7 bn | 29% × US$36.8 bn (rounded) |
| Premium/FIT share of South India inbound | 35–50% | Independent/curated travelers, wellness seekers, HNW families |
| Domestic premium overlay | +US$2–4 bn | High-net-worth Indian travelers to South India |
| SAM | US$8–12 bn | Premium inbound + domestic premium FIT/leisure in South India |
| santos.travel attainable share | 0.05–0.15% of SAM | Boutique operator with strong digital/AI-driven distribution |
| SOM | US$5–15 mn/year | Near-term realistic revenue capture |

### 5.2 Calculations

#### TAM — Total Addressable Market

```
TAM = Total international visitor spending in India
    = US$36.8 billion (2024)
    ≈ US$37.5 billion (2025 projected)
```

This is the broadest possible market: all foreign money spent on travel and tourism in India, including business, leisure, medical, MICE, VFR and religious travel.

#### SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

```
SAM = Premium/FIT leisure spending in South India (inbound + domestic premium)

Inbound component:
  = South India FTA share × India inbound spend × premium/FIT factor
  = 29% × US$36.8 bn × 40% (midpoint)
  ≈ US$4.3 bn

Domestic premium component (estimated):
  ≈ US$3–7 bn

SAM range = US$8–12 bn
```

This captures travelers who:
- Visit Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh or Telangana.
- Book private, customized or small-group itineraries.
- Stay at boutique/luxury accommodations and spend on experiences.
- Are interested in wellness, heritage, backwaters, cuisine, art and nature.

#### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

```
SOM = SAM × realistic market share captured by santos.travel

Conservative: 0.05% × US$10 bn = US$5 mn
Base case:   0.10% × US$10 bn = US$10 mn
Optimistic:  0.15% × US$10 bn = US$15 mn
```

**Cross-check via trip volume:**

| Scenario | Trips/year | Avg pax/trip | Avg spend/pax | Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 250 | 2.5 | US$8,000 | US$5.0 mn |
| Base case | 400 | 2.5 | US$10,000 | US$10.0 mn |
| Optimistic | 600 | 2.5 | US$10,000 | US$15.0 mn |

A base-case SOM of **US$10 million** represents roughly:
- **400 private trips/year**
- **~1,000 guests/year**
- **~0.03% of South India's premium FIT market**

This is realistic for a digitally enabled boutique operator leveraging AI-driven quote generation, itinerary PDFs and targeted long-haul marketing.

## 6. Key Trends Relevant to santos.travel

### 6.1 Experiential & Slow Travel
- Travelers are shifting from sightseeing to **immersion**: cooking with families, village walks, artisan workshops, homestays.
- Kerala's **Responsible Tourism Mission** and community-based experiences fit this trend.

### 6.2 Ayurveda & Wellness Tourism
- India wellness tourism projected to grow from **US$19.4 bn (2024) to US$29.9 bn (2031)**.
- Kerala is the **global Ayurveda hub**; international travelers book 7–21-day Panchakarma/rejuvenation programs.
- Post-pandemic health consciousness is driving demand for nature, immunity and mental-wellness retreats.

### 6.3 Heritage & Culture
- South India has **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**: Hampi, Mahabalipuram, Western Ghats, Nilgiri Biosphere.
- Kerala offers **Kathakali, Theyyam, Kalaripayattu**, colonial heritage (Fort Kochi), Jewish/Christian/Muslim history.
- Tamil Nadu's temple architecture and living traditions attract high-yield cultural travelers.

### 6.4 Backwaters & Nature
- Kerala backwaters (Alleppey, Kumarakom, Kollam) remain iconic.
- Houseboat category is moving upscale: luxury eco-houseboats and private kettuvallams.
- Wildlife tourism (Periyar, Wayanad, Bandipur, Nagarhole) is growing among FITs.

### 6.5 Sustainable & Regenerative Tourism
- Kerala Tourism prioritizes **ecologically sustained tourism** minimizing environmental impact.
- Global luxury travelers increasingly seek **carbon-conscious, community-positive** itineraries.
- Santos.travel can differentiate via certified sustainable partners, plastic-free itineraries and direct community benefit.

### 6.6 Digital-First Booking & AI Personalization
- The India travel market is seeing rapid AI adoption (e.g., MakeMyTrip + OpenAI integration, Feb 2026).
- High-net-worth FITs expect **instant, personalized quotes**, visual itineraries and transparent pricing.
- IVA Cortex's automation (quote generation, itinerary PDFs) directly addresses this operational need.

### 6.7 Policy Tailwinds
- **Swadesh Darshan 2.0** and **PRASHAD** schemes upgrade tourism infrastructure.
- **e-Visa** expansion and improved connectivity (international airports in Kochi, Thiruvananthapuram, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad).
- **Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)-UDAN** approved 48 new seaplane routes in Kerala (2025), linking airports to backwater destinations.

## 7. Strategic Implications for santos.travel

1. **Focus on high-yield source markets:** US, UK, EU, Australia, Canada — these dominate Kerala's inbound arrivals and have high FIT propensity.
2. **Build a Kerala-plus-South-India portfolio:** Kerala as anchor, with extensions into Tamil Nadu (temples), Karnataka (Hampi, Coorg, Bandipur), and Telangana (Hyderabad).
3. **Lead with wellness and backwaters:** These are Kerala's strongest, highest-margin niches.
4. **Target 400+ private trips/year** to reach a US$10 mn revenue base.
5. **Use IVA Cortex automation** to reduce quote turnaround time and improve conversion in the premium FIT segment.
6. **Emphasize sustainability and community impact** to align with evolving luxury traveler values.
